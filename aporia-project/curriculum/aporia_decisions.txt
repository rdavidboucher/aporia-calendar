SECTION 1: PROJECT IDENTITY
================================================================================

Name: Aporia
  - Etymology: Socratic term for productive uncertainty / philosophical impasse.
  - Signals intellectual seriousness to the target user.

Tagline (working): A daily companion for the examined life.

Format: Daily companion / journal app. Not a life-hack app. Not a summary app.
  - The philosophical tradition provides the frame.
  - The user provides the content through daily writing.
  - The real goal: use entries to remind the user of books they have read, then
    generate thinking and writing grounded in their own life — not lived through
    other thinkers' lenses.

Target user: The user themselves (solo project at MVP stage). Intellectually
  serious, has read widely, codes in Python, not a beginner.

SECTION 2: THE DAILY LOOP (CORE STRUCTURE)
================================================================================

Each day consists of four modules, in order:

--- MODULE I: THE MAIN ENTRY (New Material) ---

  Fields:
  - Title: (string) Evocative title for the day's entry.
  - Thinkers: (array of strings) 1-2 primary thinkers for the day.
  - The Seed: A direct quote or core idea. Must follow Quote Policy (see S7).
  - The Synthesis: Brief explanation of the underlying principle. 2-4 paragraphs.
    Written to remind the user of the book/idea, not to teach from scratch.
  - The Application: A concrete, repeatable action or exercise for that day.
    Specific enough to do before 9am if needed.
  - The Tags: One or more tags from the canonical Tag System (see Section 4).
  - The Evidence Note: A scientific "reality check" following the Evidence Policy
    (see Section 6). Format: "Research on X (Author, Year) supports Y
    directionally. [Qualifier: ...]" Must use approved qualifiers only.

--- MODULE II: THE SRS REVIEW (3-5 Entries) ---

  - A rotating stack of previous entries.
  - Scheduling follows the SM-2 algorithm (Wozniak, 1990).
  - Intervals: driven by user quality rating after each review (see Section 5).
  - Each review card contains: original Seed, a recall prompt, and the Tags.
  - The review prompts ask "how did this land in practice?" not "what did this mean?"

--- MODULE III: TODAY'S PROMPT (The Reflection Loop) ---

  - One journal prompt per day.
  - Three rotating archetypes (cycling through the year):
      * Synthetic: ties the prompt to the day's new Main Entry.
      * Audit: accountability check on a past practice entry.
      * In-The-Wild: applies a philosophical model to a random daily event.
  - The prompt must require the user to surface something from their own life.
    The thinker provides the frame. The user provides the content.
  - The Van Gogh "Letter to Theo" prompt from the Day 3 proof of concept is the
    canonical quality standard for this module.

--- MODULE IV: THE CATEGORIZED ARCHIVE ---

  - Persistent database of all user journal responses.
  - Each saved response is tagged with the Tag System labels.
  - The Archive is searchable and filterable by tag.
  - CRITICAL PRODUCT REQUIREMENT: In Arc 12 (Days 331-365), the final 10 days
    use the user's own Archive entries as the primary input for prompts — not
    the content database. The app must be able to surface past journal entries
    by tag and date as the "text" for these final prompts. This must be
    architected into the Firestore schema from the beginning, not retrofitted.

SECTION 3: THE VALIDATION / SRS RATING FIELD
================================================================================

After reading each Main Entry or SRS Review, the user rates it with a 3-point
scale. This feeds directly into the SM-2 algorithm to compute the next interval.

  Rating options (displayed as buttons in the UI):
  - "Easy"       -> maps to SM-2 quality score: 5
  - "Took effort" -> maps to SM-2 quality score: 3
  - "Struggled"  -> maps to SM-2 quality score: 1

  Additional boolean field on each Entry document:
  - reviewed: true/false
  - reviewed_at: timestamp

  SM-2 algorithm (Wozniak, 1990) governs all interval calculations.
  Do NOT use fixed intervals. Intervals are adaptive per user performance.
  Reference implementation: https://www.supermemo.com/en/archives1990-2015/english/ol/sm2

================================================================================
SECTION 4: THE TAG SYSTEM
================================================================================

Every Main Entry and every journal response must carry one or more of these tags.
Tags are the connective tissue of the entire SRS and Archive system.

  [practice]         A concrete action or exercise.
  [mindset]          A reframe or shift in perception.
  [warning]          A trap, failure mode, or danger to avoid.
  [tension]          Genuine disagreement between authors or traditions.
  [overlap]          Genuine agreement across traditions.
  [evidence-based]   Supported by empirical psychology or neuroscience.
  [speculative]      Plausible but unverified. Applied liberally per author policy.
  [internally focused] Concerns the self and inner life.
  [relational]       Concerns others and society.
  [theistic]         Requires or assumes belief in transcendence.
  [unresolved]       No synthesis is possible. Left deliberately open.

================================================================================
SECTION 5: SRS LOGIC
================================================================================

Algorithm: SM-2 (Wozniak, 1990). Adaptive intervals, not fixed.
The system combats the Forgetting Curve by scheduling reviews at the moment of
optimal forgetting — just before the memory decays.

User-facing rating scale maps to SM-2 quality scores:
  Easy         -> 5
  Took effort  -> 3
  Struggled    -> 1

Any entry rated below quality 3 is re-shown the next day regardless of interval.

Approximate interval progression for a well-rated card:
  Review 1: 1 day
  Review 2: 3 days
  Review 3: 7 days
  Review 4: 21 days
  Review 5+: computed by SM-2 ease factor, grows indefinitely

The SRS stack shown each day (Module II) contains 3-5 cards drawn from the
scheduled queue. The queue is maintained in Firestore per user per entry.

================================================================================
SECTION 6: EVIDENCE POLICY
================================================================================

NEVER present research as "proving" philosophy.

Approved qualifiers (use exactly these strings):
  - "Strong alignment"
  - "Good directional support"
  - "Speculative application"
  - "The empirical situation is currently unsettled"

Format for Evidence Notes:
  "Research on X (Author, Year) supports Y directionally.
  [Qualifier: the philosophical claim exceeds what the evidence strictly
  demonstrates.]"

Never say "studies show" or "science proves." Never present a single study as
definitive. Note replication issues where known (e.g., some behavioral economics
findings have not replicated cleanly post-2015).

================================================================================
SECTION 7: QUOTE POLICY
================================================================================

Precision is non-negotiable.

  - If a quote cannot be verified against a primary source, flag as:
      (paraphrase) or (source uncertain)
  - Never present a secondary source's paraphrase as a direct quote.
  - Prioritize primary texts in this order:
      1. The original work (Letters, Meditations, Epistles, etc.)
      2. A verified scholarly translation
      3. A reputable secondary source, flagged as (via [Author])

Special sourcing rules by author:
  - Van Gogh: Prioritize Letters to Theo. Cite by letter number or date where
    possible. Personal, applied tone is the target register.
  - Rilke: Prioritize Letters to a Young Poet. Flag other sources carefully.
  - Leonardo da Vinci: All quotes must be flagged (via Isaacson) or
    (Notebooks, paraphrase). Isaacson is a biographer, not a primary source.
    Do not present Isaacson's synthesis as Leonardo's direct voice.
  - Naval Ravikant: Default tag [speculative] on every entry. Primary "texts"
    are podcasts, Twitter threads, and The Almanack of Naval Ravikant
    (a crowd-sourced compilation). Quote verification is difficult. Flag
    all quotes as (source uncertain) unless traceable to a specific interview.
  - Rick Rubin: Default tag [speculative] on every entry. The Creative Act
    (2023) is the primary source.
  - Harari: Default tag [speculative] on most entries. Claims frequently exceed
    evidence. Note historian/scientist criticisms where relevant.
  - Matt Ridley: REMOVED from pantheon. His optimism thesis has been
    substantively criticized for cherry-picking data.

SECTION 9: THE 12-ARC YEAR STRUCTURE
================================================================================

The year is organized into four quarters, each with a governing question.
The arc structure ensures SRS relationships build coherently across the year.

OVERARCHING NARRATIVE:
  Q1 — Perception:  Who am I and how do I see?
  Q2 — Action:      How do I act well in the world?
  Q3 — Systems:     What are the forces larger than me?
  Q4 — Integration: What does it all add up to?

--- ARC 1 | Days 1-30 | Q1 ---
Title: The Foundation of Attention
Governing Question: What deserves your focus, and why does it keep slipping away?
Primary Thinkers: Marcus Aurelius, Epictetus, Thoreau, Cal Newport,
  Csikszentmihalyi, Shunryu Suzuki
Dominant Tags: [practice] [mindset] [internally focused] [overlap]
Key Tension: Newport's structured intentionality vs. Suzuki's effortless awareness
SRS Seeds (carry forward): Stoic dichotomy of control (recurs Arc 6, Arc 10).
  Csikszentmihalyi's flow (recurs Arc 4).
Closing Note: Ends with [tension] entry — is focused attention a discipline to
  be trained, or a natural state to be recovered?

--- ARC 2 | Days 31-60 | Q1 ---
Title: The Architecture of Self
Governing Question: Who is the self doing the attending?
Primary Thinkers: Kierkegaard, Sartre, de Beauvoir, Adler, William James, Epictetus
Dominant Tags: [mindset] [warning] [internally focused] [unresolved]
Key Tension: Sartre (existence precedes essence) vs. Adler (teleological self,
  you move toward goals not away from causes) vs. James (self as habit).
SRS Seeds: Bad faith (recurs Arc 10). James on habit (recurs Arc 8).
Closing Note: Ends with [unresolved] — do you construct the self, or discover it?

--- ARC 3 | Days 61-90 | Q1 ---
Title: The World as It Is
Governing Question: How reliably do you perceive reality?
Primary Thinkers: Kahneman, Taleb, Harari, Pascal, Socrates, Bevelin
Dominant Tags: [warning] [mindset] [evidence-based] [speculative] [unresolved]
Key Tension: Kahneman (bias is cognitive and partially correctable) vs. Taleb
  (the system is structurally fragile, correction is insufficient).
SRS Seeds: Taleb's antifragility (recurs Arc 6). Harari's imagined orders (recurs Arc 9).
Closing Note: Ends with Socrates — the examined life begins with admitting
  you do not know.

--- ARC 4 | Days 91-120 | Q2 ---
Title: Making Things
Governing Question: What does serious creative work require, and what stands in the way?
Primary Thinkers: van Gogh, Rilke, Pressfield, Rick Rubin, Chouinard,
  Leonardo (via Isaacson/Notebooks), Csikszentmihalyi
Dominant Tags: [practice] [warning] [internally focused] [speculative]
Key Tension: Pressfield ("ship it, the muse comes after starting") vs. Rilke
  ("wait until you must write — do not rush ripening").
SRS Seeds: Flow (recurs Arc 8). Chouinard's ethics of building (recurs Arc 5).
Note: This is the most [practice]-oriented arc and the emotional center of Q2.

--- ARC 5 | Days 121-150 | Q2 ---
Title: What Do We Owe Each Other?
Governing Question: What are your obligations beyond yourself?
Primary Thinkers: Adam Smith (TMS), Aristotle, Hannah Arendt, de Beauvoir,
  Axelrod, Robert Wright
Dominant Tags: [relational] [overlap] [tension] [evidence-based]
Key Tension: Sartre (others are the source of radical self-consciousness) vs.
  Arendt (others are the condition of meaning — the self only becomes real
  in the public realm).
SRS Seeds: Impartial spectator (recurs Arc 10). Axelrod's tit-for-tat (recurs Arc 9).
Note: First explicitly [relational]-dominant arc. Opens with Adam Smith as moral
  philosopher — the deliberate surprise of TMS vs. Wealth of Nations.

--- ARC 6 | Days 151-180 | Q2 ---
Title: The Management of Fortune
Governing Question: What do you actually control? How do you build a life robust
  to what you do not?
Primary Thinkers: Taleb, Seneca, Marcus Aurelius, Pascal, Charlie Munger,
  Ray Dalio, Montaigne
Dominant Tags: [warning] [practice] [mindset] [tension]
Key Tension: Stoic amor fati (love what happens, accept fully) vs. Taleb's
  antifragility (engineer conditions so bad outcomes make you stronger — active,
  not merely accepting).
SRS Seeds: Munger's inversion (recurs Arc 8). Montaigne's flexibility (recurs Arc 11).
Note: Hinge arc of the year. Readers have examined self, built self, looked at
  creative work, confronted obligations. Now: what happens when the plan fails?

--- ARC 7 | Days 181-210 | Q3 ---
Title: The Body and Time
Governing Question: You live in a body that will die. What does philosophy say?
Primary Thinkers: Epicurus, Gawande, Seneca, Montaigne, William James, Tolstoy
Dominant Tags: [warning] [mindset] [unresolved] [internally focused]
Key Tension: Epicurus (remove death-anxiety as precondition for living freely)
  vs. Tolstoy's Ivan Ilyich (the deathbed realization that you lived the wrong
  life — fiction as philosophy).
SRS Seeds: Seneca on time (carries as [warning] throughout Q4).
  Gawande on systems (recurs Arc 8).
Note: Deliberately avoids Heidegger (prose difficulty too high for daily format).
  Tolstoy's Death of Ivan Ilyich does the same philosophical work in narrative.

--- ARC 8 | Days 211-240 | Q3 ---
Title: Systems, Performance, and the Long Game
Governing Question: How do you build structures that reliably produce good outcomes?
Primary Thinkers: Hamming, Gawande, Newport, Bevelin, William James, Munger
Dominant Tags: [practice] [evidence-based] [overlap]
Key Tension: Hamming ("work on the important problem" — prioritize ruthlessly)
  vs. Newport ("protect deep work at all costs" — structure ruthlessly).
  Compatible emphases but create real daily planning tension.
SRS Seeds: Hamming's "important problem" question (recurs as Q4 synthesis prompt).
  James on habit (final synthesis in Arc 12).
Note: Most explicitly applied/performance arc. Hamming's 1986 Bell Labs lecture
  is the empirical anchor (Nobel laureate habit study). Bevelin's Seeking Wisdom
  is the most underread book on the full list.

--- ARC 9 | Days 241-270 | Q3 ---
Title: Society, Power, and Collective Fictions
Governing Question: What stories is civilization telling you, and are you choosing
  them consciously?
Primary Thinkers: Harari, Arendt, Nietzsche, Axelrod, Wright, Adam Smith (TMS
  revisited), Confucius, Laozi
Dominant Tags: [tension] [warning] [relational] [unresolved] [speculative]
Key Tension: Nietzsche (all moral systems are power-projects — be suspicious) vs.
  Axelrod/Wright (cooperation is empirically stable and evolutionarily favored,
  giving morality a non-theistic foundation).
SRS Seeds: Nietzsche's revaluation (becomes [unresolved] thread in Arc 11).
  Wright's cooperation (becomes [overlap] resolution in Arc 12).
Note: Most politically complex arc. Deliberately places Harari's imagined orders
  at civilizational scale (Arc 3 was personal cognitive scale). Nietzsche's
  genealogy of morality is the most challenging entry of the year.

--- ARC 10 | Days 271-300 | Q4 ---
Title: The Dark Night
Governing Question: What do the philosophers say when things actually fall apart?
Primary Thinkers: Frankl, Kierkegaard, Camus, Nietzsche, de Beauvoir, Seneca,
  Marcus Aurelius
Dominant Tags: [warning] [mindset] [unresolved] [internally focused]
Key Tension: Frankl (meaning is available even in suffering — the will to meaning
  is primary) vs. Camus (there is no meaning — only the choice to revolt against
  meaninglessness, which is itself the meaning).
SRS Seeds: Frankl's will to meaning (becomes [overlap] resolution in Arc 12
  alongside Aristotle's eudaimonia).
Note: PLACEMENT IS INTENTIONAL. Frankl encountered at Day 5 = motivational quote.
  Frankl encountered at Day 271 after 270 days of examined material = full
  philosophical weight. Marcus Aurelius writing Meditations — not for publication,
  at war, probably sick, facing children's deaths — is the proof of concept for
  everything Arc 1 built.

--- ARC 11 | Days 301-330 | Q4 ---
Title: Wisdom Traditions in Dialogue
Governing Question: What happens when you put all the traditions in the same room?
Primary Thinkers: Suzuki, Laozi, Confucius, Montaigne, Epicurus, Aristotle,
  Russell, Zweig
Dominant Tags: [overlap] [tension] [unresolved] [theistic]
Key Tension: Eastern non-attachment (the good life requires releasing desire) vs.
  Aristotle's eudaimonia (the good life requires exercising your highest
  capacities — deeply appetitive, not renunciatory).
SRS Seeds: This arc seeds no new concepts. Designed to trigger the broadest
  possible SRS review stack from the full year.
Note: Explicitly surfaces [overlap] and [tension] patterns across traditions that
  the tag system has been quietly building all year. Russell's Conquest of
  Happiness is the 20th-century humanist synthesis attempt.

--- ARC 12 | Days 331-365 | Q4 ---
Title: The Examined Life
Governing Question: What does the year add up to?
Primary Thinkers: Russell, Montaigne, Thoreau, Tolstoy, Aristotle, James, Frankl
Dominant Tags: [overlap] [practice] [unresolved]
Structure:
  Days 331-355 (25 entries): Explicit synthesis. Each entry pairs two thinkers
    from different arcs and asks what they would say to each other.
  Days 356-365 (10 entries): Journal-forward. Seed and Synthesis compressed.
    Application and Today's Prompt expanded. The user's own Archive entries
    (pulled by tag and date from Firestore) are the primary input — not the
    content database. This is the proof of the project's real goal.
  Day 365: Single prompt, no thinker attached. "What is your examined life?"
Final Tension: Montaigne (the essay is never finished, only abandoned) vs.
  Aristotle (the telos of a life can be assessed at its end). Left open.
  Deliberately. Final entry is always [unresolved].

SECTION 10: TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE
================================================================================

MVP Target: Progressive Web App (PWA)
  Rationale: Fastest path to iPhone-accessible product. Validate the daily loop
  with real use before building native. iOS PWA support is second-class vs.
  Android (background sync, notifications, offline behavior inconsistent on
  Safari) but acceptable for MVP.
  Escalation path: React Native or Flutter if native iOS performance is required
  after MVP validation. Do not build native until the daily loop is validated
  over 30 real days.

Stack:
  Frontend:   React (PWA)
  Hosting:    Render.com
  Database:   Firebase Firestore
  Schema:     JSON Schema (define before writing any content — schema integrity
              determines tagging/filtering reliability)

Firestore Document Structure (minimum required fields per Entry):
  entry_id:         string (unique)
  day_number:       integer
  arc_number:       integer (1-12)
  title:            string
  thinkers:         array of strings
  seed:             string
  seed_source:      string (include (paraphrase) or (source uncertain) flags here)
  synthesis:        string
  application:      string
  tags:             array of strings (from canonical Tag System)
  evidence_note:    string (null if not applicable)
  prompt:           string
  prompt_archetype: string (Synthetic | Audit | In-The-Wild)
  created_at:       timestamp
  reviewed:         boolean
  reviewed_at:      timestamp (null until reviewed)
  srs_quality:      integer (1, 3, or 5 — from user rating)
  srs_interval:     integer (days, computed by SM-2)
  srs_ease_factor:  float (SM-2 ease factor, initial value 2.5)
  srs_next_review:  timestamp (computed)
  srs_repetitions:  integer (SM-2 repetition count)

Firestore Document Structure (minimum required fields per Journal Response):
  response_id:      string (unique)
  entry_id:         string (foreign key to Entry)
  day_number:       integer
  arc_number:       integer
  prompt_archetype: string
  response_text:    string
  tags:             array of strings (user-confirmed or auto-inherited from entry)
  created_at:       timestamp

CRITICAL: Archive query capability must support filtering by tag AND date range.
  This is required for Arc 12 (Days 356-365) where past journal entries are
  the primary content input, not the philosophical database.

SRS UI:
  After reading each entry or SRS review card, user sees three buttons:
    "Easy"        -> srs_quality = 5
    "Took effort" -> srs_quality = 3
    "Struggled"   -> srs_quality = 1
  Any entry rated below quality 3 returns to queue the next day regardless of
  computed interval.

SECTION 11: CONTENT PRODUCTION PIPELINE
================================================================================

Phase 1: Content before code.
  Write and review 30 days of content at full quality before writing app code.
  Validate that the daily loop actually works for the user over 30 real days.

Phase 2: Write in 30-day batches (one arc at a time).
  User reviews each arc before the next is written.
  Quality standard: The Day 3 proof of concept (Rilke + Camus / Solitude and
  the Absurd) is the canonical benchmark for entry quality.

Content writing rules:
  - The Application and Today's Prompt are the product. The Seed and Synthesis
    are the ramp.
  - Prompts must require the user to surface something from their own life.
    The thinker provides the frame. The user provides the content.
  - Never present entries as teaching material. The user has read these books.
    The entry reminds and activates, it does not instruct.
  - Thinker frequency cap: no single thinker as primary in more than 3 arcs.
    (Any thinker may appear in SRS review material across the full year.)

Current status: Year arc fully mapped. Ready to write Arc 1 (Days 1-30).
  Arc 1 primary thinkers: Marcus Aurelius, Epictetus, Thoreau, Cal Newport,
  Csikszentmihalyi, Shunryu Suzuki.
