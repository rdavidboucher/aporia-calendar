--- THE SYNTHESIS ---

Day 348 continues the retrospective accounting with the relational dimension —
the specific obligations that the dying-reveal evidence and the dying's imagining
generate for the specific relationships the examined life holds.

The relational accounting has three registers — the same structure that Arc 9's
political obligations and Arc 11's love obligations used:

and the specific acknowledgments and the specific reconciliations that the dying-
reveal evidence identifies as the most common relational regrets. These are
the most urgent obligations: the things the examined life intends to do tomorrow

freedom-willing that is not yet fully practiced, the unselfing that the distortion
audit has identified as most needed, the specific attention that the love's

to know — the specific account of what they have meant, what the love has built,
what the examined life has held in the interior space of the dark. Not the letter
(which comes on Day 358) — the honest accounting of what the legacy of the
relationship most importantly consists in.

--- THE SYNTHESIS ---

Day 349 applies the dying's confrontation to the work — the creative practice
that Arc 8 examined as the making that sustains the somehow-on and that has
been present throughout as one of the column-one candidates for the examined
life's most important somehow-ons.

The work-and-death question: the creative practice faces the dying in two
dimensions. The first is the dying's interruption — the work that the dying
will prevent from being completed, the making that the somehow-on will not
have enough time to sustain. The second is the work's relationship to the
dying's meaning — the degree to which the work's continuing is itself the
examined life's most significant form of the somehow-on, the making that
most directly constitutes the column-one interior that the dying's imagining
has confirmed.

The dying-reveal evidence applied to the work: the deathbed regret research
suggests that work regrets are less commonly expressed than relational regrets —
but the work regrets that do appear are consistently regrets of the work not
undertaken rather than the work not completed. The examined life does not regret
finishing at the wrong place in the project. It regrets not having begun the
project most worth beginning.

The practical implication: what is the work most worth beginning that the examined
life has not yet begun? The project that the dying's confrontation identifies
as most important to have undertaken — not the most ambitious project or the
most philosophically impressive, the most genuinely owned project, the work
that is most specifically the examined life's ownmost making.

--- THE APPLICATION ---

life that the somehow-on has not yet produced.

the work that the dying's imagining confirms as genuinely present in the interior

--- THE SEED ---

--- THE SYNTHESIS ---

Day 350 is the design entry for Arc 12 — the equivalent of Day 287's building-
for-the-dark and Day 323's design for love. The design for the dying is the
twelfth section of the design document: the specific practices that build the
examined life's capacity for the most honest confrontation with its own dying.

The design has six components — one drawing from each primary source:

The Montaigne practice: the specific regular imaginative engagement with the
dying — the weekly or daily return to the specific imagining of the examined
life's own dying, in its specific aspects, as the most direct practice of the
learning-to-die. Not the awareness of the death's certainty — the specific
imagining of the dying itself, in the Montaigne mode.

The Epicurean practice: the specific regular engagement with the Epicurean
argument — the deliberate holding of the argument that death is not an experience
the examined life can have, used as the specific practice that most directly
addresses the fear of non-existence. The practice of distinguishing the fear
of non-existence from the grief of the goods the continued life would produce,
and applying the argument specifically to the former.

The Heideggerian practice: the specific regular confrontation with the ownmost
possibility — the holding of all five characters (proper, unconditional, certain,
indefinite, not to be outstripped) in the deliberate mode, as the practice that
most directly builds the anticipatory resoluteness and most directly individualizes
the existence. Separate from the anxiety that arrives unbidden — this is the
practiced confrontation.

The Kübler-Ross practice: the specific regular engagement with the stages as
descriptions of the dying process — the practice of sitting with each stage's
specific interior formation, not as the prediction of the examined life's dying
but as the preparation of the interior for the stages' possible arrivals.

The relational practice: the specific daily behaviors that close the gap between
the knowing and the acting in the most significant relationships — the immediate
obligations implemented before the dying, as the most direct form of the dying's
preparation in the relational domain.

The column-one building: the specific ongoing practice that builds the most
genuinely column-one good — the good identified in Day 346's retrospective
accounting as the single most column-one good — toward the most robust possible
presence in the dying's condition.

--- THE APPLICATION ---

which one has the least developed specific practice — the one the design specifies

for the least developed component. That when-then is the design's completion —
and the most important practical output of the retrospective accounting sequence.

preparation has been sufficient.
